Alpha  

Overview

In alpha teams try out different solutions to the problems they learnt about during discovery. We spent alpha building prototypes and testing different ideas.

Initial prototype

What we did

  • Produced a prototype in Balsamiq (as an interaction designer had not yet joined the team)
  • Initial hypotheses were defined and prioritised in various workshops
  • A journey was mapped and defined based on our discovery research
  • The prototype reflected this journey and otherwise followed the GOV.UK design system and content design guidelines
  • The order of the design approximated the order of the paper calculation sheet

Our core hypotheses

  1. WE BELIEVE

    IF we show a full sentence calculation breakdown at the end of the journey

    FOR all members of staff

    THEN this will help them trust the service

    We would know this is true when staff accept dates calculated by the service

  2. WE BELIEVE

    IF we create a mandatory list of questions

    FOR all members of staff

    THEN we know they are considering all necessary information

    We would know this is true when when experienced members of staff feel everything necessary has been considered

1st prototype (never tested)

  • Remake of initial prototype in code using the prototyping kit

Tech pivot

Our tech team discovered that writing information to NOMIS carried a large risk. As such, it was decided that inputting data would be removed from the scope of our service. Our service would calculate release dates based on information in NOMIS. This changed the overall journey and the hypotheses we would test.

  1. WE BELIEVE

    IF we show the user everything currently inputted into NOMIS to review before the calculation is done

    FOR all users with a calculator mindset

    THEN the user can feel confident we have taken the specific information from NOMIS into account with the calculation

    AND gives them the opportunity to spot a mistake to fix in NOMIS before the calculation is done

    We would know this is true when

    • We understand how users feel about adapting any mistakes on NOMIS and returning to our service
    • We understand if users having to pick closest to in NOMIS impacts their sense of trust for our service
    • Users feel we have captured all necessary information from NOMIS to complete the calculation

  2. WE BELIEVE

    IF we show a full sentence calculation breakdown at the end of the journey

    FOR all members of staff

    THEN this will help them trust the service

    We would know this is true when staff accept dates calculated by the service

4th prototype iteration (not yet tested)

Changes made in this prototype:

  • The types of sentences covered in Alpha are emphasised on start page.
  • We included a message saying that some detail not included in calculation based on offender circumstances - such as HDC eligibility - emphasised at start of calculation
  • Added option to print calculation
  • Reordered calculation summary to prioritise release dates, based on findings from user research
  • Removed some information included in the calculation summary that duplicated that on the previous page, based in user research findings
  • Revised check NOMIS screen to accommodate longer lists of sentences
  • Added additional journeys for cancelling a calculation and reporting a problem
  • Added banner to explain multiple offences for single sentences being automatically split into multiple sentences by our service
  • Added errors for anticipated problems
